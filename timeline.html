The start of K-pop can be pinpointed almost exactly unlike for many other music genres. Seo Taiji was a member of a South Korean heavy metal band and after their disbandment, he turned to hip-hop. He made a small group with two stunning dancers, namely Yang Hyun-suk, and Lee Juno. With their Debut in 1992, the group “Seo Taiji and Boys” performed at a talent show with the song “Nan Arayo” meaning ‘I know’. Though the group got the lowest score of the evening, the song went to the top of South Korea’s singles charts for 17 weeks, holding the record for more than 15 years later as the longest #1 streak.

Seo Taiji and Boys challenged all norms and sang about real issues that students and adults alike were facing within the strict schooling system and work environment. After creating all their music for the next few years, they disbanded in 1996. The legendary group paved the way for other artists and companies to be more experimental and out of the box.
